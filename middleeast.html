history

how it's made

Booza is often considered to be the worlds oldest ice cream. It originated in the eastern part of the Mediterranean at around 1400 AD (Steussy). This encompasses the modern day levant region (Khoury-Hanold). Booza's original flavour was Ashta, for the Lebanese and Qashta, for the Syrians (Ramzi).

Booza is a ice cream that is stretchable in the same consistency as taffy, or fresh mozzarella (Khoury-Hanold). Booza is able to stretch while still staying soft as it is made with Sahlab which is ground orchid root, and mastic, which is a common ingredient in chewing gum (Khoury-Hanold). Traditionally, only milk is used besides these ingredients (Ramzi). Booza is also not churned as other ice creams are, but put in an ice-cold drum where it is first pounded with a wooden pestle before it is pulled and stretched by hand (Khoury-Hanold).
